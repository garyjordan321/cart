Cartel Storms Mazatlán Animal Shelter, Kills One Dog in Chaotic Attack

November 20, 2024

    In a disturbing act of violence, cartel members stormed an animal shelter in Mazatlán, Sinaloa, late last night. The group reportedly terrorized the facility, freeing the animals in a chaotic rampage. Amid the destruction, they fatally shot one dog, Solana, before fleeing the scene.

    Shelter staff described Solana as a calm presence among the rescued animals, making her death particularly harrowing. Other dogs escaped in the chaos and remain missing. Authorities are investigating the motive, with speculation ranging from intimidation tactics to an isolated act of cruelty.

    The incident has fueled calls for better security for animal welfare organizations, as the community mourns the tragic loss and searches for the displaced animals.

    He recalled that these are added to those already in place to and from Los Angeles, Houston and Phoenix, among other routes.

    In the case of domestic flights, and others that change days, the head of the Sectur Sinaloa said that, in the case of the flight to El Bajío with Volaris, starting this month it will be on Fridays and Mondays, when previously it was on Saturdays and Mondays, which is an opportunity for users to take advantage of the weekend at the port.

    The meeting of the Airport Advisory Commission was attended by the director of OMA Mazatlán, Eduardo Galindo Gómez; the head of Sedectur, Celia Jáuregui Ibarra, as well as representatives of the hotel associations and business chambers of Mazatlán.
